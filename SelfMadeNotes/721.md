**Lemma: 721.1: Polynomials representing boolean functions have sum decomposition**

Any polynomial $p \in \mathsf{PolyApprox}$, can be written as a linear combination of 2^n monomials where each monomial is determined by a subset sampled from $[n]$. Concretelty,

$$\forall p \in \mathsf{PolyApprox}\colon \quad p = \sum_{S \in [n]}^{2^n} \alpha_S \cdot X(m_S)$$

Where $m_s = \sum_{i \in S} f_i$ and $\alpha_{s} \in \real$ (the scalarcoffecient).

And $f_i\colon [n] \mapsto \set{0,1}$ is the function that sends $i$ to 1 and all other values to zero.
